HELP REFUGEES ALLIANCE

Help Refugees Alliance is a digital platform that connects volunteers worldwide willing to offer refugees a new home. It has also ranked as one of the leading international non-governmental organisations aimed at giving assistance to refugees. Other organisations such as Amnesty International and Refugees Foundation have full access to our database.

ABOUT US

The organisation was founded during the 2016 refugee crisis by a group of five families in Germany. At that time those families decided to host at their homes 20 families of refugees. Today we are about 2,000 families connected worldwide.

  • Through our volunteer work you will not only be sharing love with those who need assistance in critical times, but also promoting understanding among people with different cultural backgrounds.
  • When opening ourselves to closely experience each other's culture, we have the chance to learn something new, as well as be aware of the circunstances which lead refugees to flee from their countries, e.g. war and civil war, human rights violations, environment and climate and/or economic hardship.
  • This will give you a deep sense of humanity and make you to pay attention to other issues that have been affecting the whole world for decades.
  • We always stress to our host families that they must respect refugees' personal boundaries and give them support with their integration into a new culture. Any sort of cultural enforcement is disallowed.
  • Psychological and medical support is offered by our health team.
  • Labor relations with refugees are forbidden. We are here to offer love!

OUR MISSION

We are aimed to ensure that housing must be viewed as a human right which cannot be violated. Due to economic and social crisis housing has increasingly been treated as a commodity. Our ambition is to spread around the globe the following message — the world is everyone's home and everyone has the right to live in it with dignity.

  • Armed conflict, climate change, natural disasters, infrastructure development, hydro-power dams, and mega-events, such as Olympic Games or football World Cups pose a threat to the enjoyment of the right to adequate housing and displace every year millions.
  • The implementation of this human right must be done by states through government policies and programmes. If there are no public initiatives regarding this topic, we are here to take action.
  • Women, religious and ethnic minorities, indigenous peoples, persons with disabilities, migrants and refugees face discrimination in relation to housing or live in the most appalling conditions.
  • Adequate housing was recognized as part of the right to an adequate standard of living in article 25 of the 1948 Universal Declaration of Human Rights and in article 11.1 of the 1966 International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.
